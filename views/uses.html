Server

Because of the relationship of GNU/Linux with Unix, GNU/Linux has very quickly established itself in the computer server market. A crucial point has been the possibility to use a Unix-like operating system on PC-compatible hardware, which is much cheaper than proprietary Unix and hardware-specific solutions. As many popular and widely used server software products (HTTP server, database, groupware, e-mail server, etc.) are available free of charge, in general without any limitation, and reliable, the market share of GNU/Linux in this area has consequently grown rapidly.

Network Security

Linux, which has a good reputation for security and performance (scaling), is widely used in the field of computer networks, for example as a gateway, router, proxy or firewall.

Super computers

Supercomputers are designed to achieve the highest possible performance with known technologies, especially in terms of computing speed. In November 2006, according to TOP500, GNU/Linux runs 74% of the world's 500 most powerful computers (compared to 20% for UNIX), including the two IBM Blue Gene servers (40,960 and 131,072 processors). In November 2007, in the same TOP500, more than 85 per cent of supercomputers were using GNU/Linux, compared to 6 per cent for UNIX and 1.20 per cent for Windows. In November 2017, 100 per cent of the TOP500 were using GNU/Linux as their operating system.

Embedded systems

Linux is also found at the heart of many computer and consumer electronics devices, sometimes without the user's knowledge. These include network equipment and small digital devices intended for mass consumption, usually equipped with a specialized energy-efficient processor and flash memory.
